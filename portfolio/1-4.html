It’s surprising to me that Geocities interacted with the Internet as if it was an RPG game or a website forum, with its countless possibilities and co-connected webpages. For it to be taken down has got me thinking about the state of the “archive,” especially the digital one. The Internet, especially the early version of it, almost seems like the deepest depths of the ocean—now untouchable with time. But, at one point, it was the most accessible thing.
1. How can we deal with loss and grieve the corrupted information of the bygone Internet, or bygone Geocities?
2. What do we make of the future of webpages?
