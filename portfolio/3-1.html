Soulellis speaks to the indeterminacy of the feed—that its never-ending status compels the viewer to dive in forever, even in the most participatory of ways. I think of the ways in which the Internet has changed over time, granting individuals access to personal expression in the ‘90s-‘00s-era blog website, into the corporatization and aesthetic commodification of the world’s webpages. As Soulellis argues, there’s some resistant quality to archiving components of the Internet, such that it renders a part of it permanent in the field of always-changing algorithms and feed make-ups.
